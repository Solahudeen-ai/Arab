Frequently Asked Questions

1. Do I need to know any Arabic to take this course?
Absolutely not. The program is designed specifically for non-Arabic speaking parents. We teach you how to audit and track progress using rubrics that don't require you to be fluent.
2. What age groups is this framework suitable for?
The monitoring frameworks are highly adaptable and work best for parents with children aged 5 to 16, covering primary through early secondary education stages.
3. My child attends a standard Islamic school, not private tutoring. Will this help?
Yes. Whether your child is in a formal Madrasah, receiving private tutoring, or learning online, these checklists allow you to audit the effectiveness of whatever curriculum they are currently enrolled in.
4. How does the Premium direct assessment work?
Premium members can record brief audio clips of their child reading or speaking Arabic and submit them via our private portal. Our academic team at Arab Community of Lagos will review the audio and provide you with a detailed, professional progress report on their exact level.
5. Can both parents access the course portal on one purchase?
Yes. A single enrollment grants access for the household. We encourage both spouses to review the frameworks together to ensure unified academic support at home.
6. Is this a one-time fee or a monthly subscription?
All tracks for the Parents & Guardians course are a single, one-time payment for complete access.
